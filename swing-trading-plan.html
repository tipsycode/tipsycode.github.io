Data-Driven Profitable Swing Trading Plan Framework

A structured operating system for aggressive swing trading: when to focus long, when to consider short-side or puts, which sectors deserve attention, and how to control risk without trading randomly.

Important: This is an educational trading framework, not financial advice. It is designed to improve process quality, risk control, and decision discipline. It does not guarantee profits.
Core Rule

Regime Comes First

Do not start with a random chart. Start with market regime, then sector leadership, then stock relative strength, then the entry setup.

Objective

Aggressive, Not Reckless

Aggressive growth comes from pressing high-quality opportunities when conditions support risk, not from oversizing weak setups.

Bias

Conditional, Not Permanent

The plan is not always bullish or bearish. Long, short, puts, or cash depend on evidence from rates, volatility, breadth, leadership, and liquidity.

Part I

The Four-Layer Decision Stack

Every trade must pass through a hierarchy. A good-looking setup in a bad regime is not the same as a good trade.

1. Market Regime

Is the broad environment supportive of risk-taking? Check rates, dollar, volatility, breadth, and index structure.

2. Sector / Theme Leadership

Is money flowing into the sector or theme? Sector strength creates tailwind and institutional support.

3. Stock Quality & Relative Strength

Is the stock outperforming its index and sector? Strong stocks should act well before the entry trigger.

4. Setup & Execution

Is there a clean entry, stop, target area, and reward-to-risk structure? If not, the trade is not ready.

Part II

Market Regime Framework

The regime determines whether the trader should focus on long setups, short-side setups, puts, or mostly cash.

Green

Risk Expansion

Conditions support long exposure and aggressive growth trading.

  • Rates stable or falling; dollar stable or weakening.
  • Volatility compressing; breadth expanding.
  • Growth, semiconductors, AI infrastructure, and high-beta leadership improving.
  • Breakouts follow through and pullbacks are bought.

Bias: Long-focused. Use breakouts, pullbacks in leaders, continuation setups, and measured position scaling into strength.

Yellow

Neutral / Choppy

Conditions are mixed. This is where both bulls and bears can get trapped.

  • Indexes range-bound; leadership rotates quickly.
  • Rates unstable but not disorderly; volatility elevated but controlled.
  • Breadth mixed; breakouts work inconsistently.

Bias: Selective. Use smaller size, favor pullbacks or reclaims over chasing, and take profits faster.

Red

Risk Contraction

Financial conditions are restrictive and risk appetite is weak.

  • Rates elevated or rising; dollar strengthening.
  • Volatility elevated or expanding; breadth weak.
  • Defensives or energy leading while high-beta growth underperforms.
  • Breakouts fail, rallies fade, former leaders lose support.

Bias: Defensive. Reduce size, avoid aggressive dip buying, focus only on relative strength longs or structured short/put setups.

Part III

Long vs Short-Side Decision Framework

The plan should make direction conditional. The trader should not force long trades in a deteriorating tape or short strong stocks without structure.

FocusMarket ConditionsSector ConditionsBest Setups
Long / Calls Indexes above key moving averages, volatility compressing, breadth improving, rates stable/falling. Sector outperforming, multiple names confirming strength, leadership broadening. Pullback to rising support, breakout from tight consolidation, reclaim of key level, post-earnings hold.
Short / Puts Indexes below key moving averages, rallies failing, volatility rising, breadth deteriorating. Sector underperforming, multiple stocks losing support, leadership rotating away. Failed breakout, breakdown from range, lower-high rejection, bear flag, failed retest.
Cash / Wait Signals mixed, indexes chopping, volatility unpredictable, no clean reward-to-risk. Leadership rotates daily or no sector has convincing strength/weakness. No trade. Cash preserves optionality and protects the account from low-quality decisions.
Part IV

Sector Focus by Regime

Sector selection matters because stocks usually move best when they have sector tailwind.

Green

Risk Expansion Sectors

  • Semiconductors
  • AI infrastructure
  • Software and cybersecurity
  • High-beta technology
  • Growth consumer discretionary
  • Crypto-linked equities when Bitcoin and liquidity align
Yellow

Selective Sectors

  • Mega-cap technology leaders
  • Select semiconductor leaders
  • AI infrastructure names holding trend
  • Energy if oil is firm
  • Defensive growth and best-in-class earnings leaders
Red

Defensive / Short-Side Focus

  • Longs: energy, healthcare, staples, utilities, strongest relative-strength names.
  • Shorts/puts: weak high-beta tech, consumer discretionary, unprofitable growth, failed momentum leaders.
Part V

Setup Playbook

Use a small number of repeatable setups. Consistency matters more than trying to trade every pattern.

Pullback to Support

Best for: GREEN regimes and strong sectors. The trend is intact, volume cools on pullback, and price holds a prior breakout level or rising moving average.

Breakout from Consolidation

Best for: strong momentum environments. Requires tight range, clear resistance, volume expansion, and supportive breadth.

Reclaim Setup

Best for: YELLOW regimes and transition periods. Price loses a key level, quickly reclaims it, and holds above it.

Failed Breakout Short / Put

Best for: RED regimes. Price breaks above resistance, fails, closes back under the level, and traps late buyers.

Breakdown from Range

Best for: weak sectors and former leaders losing trend. Requires support break, relative weakness, and preferably a failed retest.

No Trade Setup

Best for: unclear conditions. If entry, stop, target, and regime fit are not clear, skipping is the highest-quality decision.

Part VI

Risk Management System

Risk management is what separates aggressive trading from reckless trading.

RegimePosition SizeProfit BehaviorRisk Behavior
GREENFull size allowed on A+ setups.Let winners work; trail stops; add only to winners.Risk can increase modestly, but only with clean invalidation.
YELLOWHalf-size default.Take profits faster; respect resistance.Avoid overtrading and chasing.
REDSmall size only.Take profits aggressively; expect failed follow-through.No averaging down; stops must be respected immediately.
Hard rule: Every trade must have a defined invalidation level before entry. If the stop is not clear, the trade is not ready.
Part VII

Options / Puts Framework

Options can accelerate returns, but they require stricter discipline because time decay and volatility pricing can punish hesitation.

When Calls Make Sense

  • Market regime is GREEN or improving YELLOW.
  • Sector is leading and stock has a clean breakout or reclaim.
  • Volatility is not excessively expensive.
  • There is enough time to expiration for the thesis to develop.

When Puts Make Sense

  • Market regime is RED or deteriorating YELLOW.
  • Stock breaks support or rejects a declining moving average.
  • Sector is weak and breadth is deteriorating.
  • Risk is defined and the stock is not already deeply extended downward.
Options rule: Do not average down losing options. Exit when the setup fails, not when the option becomes nearly worthless.
Part VIII

Daily Workflow

The workflow keeps the trader consistent before, during, and after the session.

Pre-Market

  • Classify regime.
  • Check rates, dollar, oil, volatility, and futures.
  • Identify leading and lagging sectors.
  • Build long and short watchlists.
  • Define entry, stop, target, and size.

During Market

  • Wait for planned triggers.
  • Avoid impulse entries.
  • Do not add to losers.
  • Watch breadth and sector confirmation.
  • Respect the regime.

After Market

  • Review whether the trade followed the plan.
  • Log regime, sector, setup, entry, exit, and mistake.
  • Grade process quality, not just profit/loss.
Part IX

Master Checklist Before Every Trade

Regime: Does the market environment support this trade direction?
Rates and volatility: Are they helping or hurting the setup?
Sector: Is the sector leading or lagging, and does that align with the trade?
Stock: Is it showing relative strength for longs or relative weakness for shorts?
Setup: Is this a pullback, breakout, reclaim, failed breakout, or breakdown?
Risk: Where is the stop, what is the target, and is reward-to-risk acceptable?
Size: Is position size appropriate for the regime?
Mindset: Is the trade planned, or is it emotional?
Final Summary

The Operating Principle

The goal is to take risk when the market is rewarding risk, reduce risk when the market is punishing risk, and remain patient when conditions are unclear.
